# Tyler Wolf

Infrastructure Engineer for Crypto Protocols

I work with crypto and Web3 infrastructure teams on the systems that actually have to work — node operations, distributed backends, protocol-level infrastructure, and the reliability engineering that keeps them running in production.

## Profile

Most of this work is at the infrastructure layer — the part that doesn't get celebrated until it breaks. I've designed and operated bare-metal Kubernetes clusters running nodes across multiple blockchain networks, built multi-tenant backend platforms on GCP, and worked as the senior technical owner on systems where correctness and uptime weren't optional. Before going independent, I ran internal platforms at King (Candy Crush), was a technical co-founder of an early SaaS company, and worked on blockchain identity applications when most people hadn't heard the word.

Through my company, Woven Signal, I work with protocols and infrastructure-layer companies that are past the experimental stage — where the system is in production, the stakes are real, and someone needs to own the hard parts. That might mean taking over architectural direction, stabilizing something that's grown beyond its original design, or building something net-new that needs to be right from the start.

We take on a small number of clients at a time and work directly with founders and senior technical stakeholders. If you're building serious infrastructure and need a professional that has operated at that level, let's talk.

## What we're typically brought in to do

- Take ownership of backend systems with meaningful complexity or scale, from early design through long-term operation
- Stabilize and evolve existing systems — improving reliability, scalability, and operational clarity
- Design and operate production infrastructure across cloud-native and decentralized environments, including protocol and blockchain node infrastructure
- Untangle legacy architectures and technical debt, establishing clear paths toward more sustainable systems
- Provide technical leadership across teams, owning architectural direction and guiding long-term technical and operational decisions
